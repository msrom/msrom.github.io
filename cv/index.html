About

I am a PhD student at the Computer Security Lab (COSEC) of Universidad Carlos III de Madrid. Before joining the lab, I worked at CSIC on cryptography, digital twins, and privacy-preserving systems, and I continue to combine research with teaching and academic leadership at UNIE. My research sits at the intersection of cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, and cyber threat intelligence, with a focus on Advanced Persistent Threats. I develop graph- and learning-based methods that leverage system-level data provenance to detect, explain, and evaluate host-based APT activity, including causality-preserving metrics for provenance-based intrusion detection systems. In parallel, I explore CTI problems around threat-actor characterization, attribution, and the normalization of inconsistent naming taxonomies across vendors, using large-scale TTP and open-source intelligence signals.
